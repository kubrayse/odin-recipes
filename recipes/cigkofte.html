Çiğ Köfte

Description

This vegan version of Turkish çiğ köfte is a delicious, spicy bulgur-based dish traditionally kneaded by hand — but in this version, a food processor does the hard work! Fine bulgur is soaked and blended with tomato paste, onions, olive oil, and a generous mix of spices like isot (Urfa pepper) and chili flakes. Pomegranate molasses and lemon juice give it a tangy depth, while the mixture is processed into a smooth, cohesive dough. Once shaped into small köfte portions, it's served with lettuce, lemon, and a glass of ayran (yogurt drink). It's a flavorful and healthy street-food favorite in Turkish cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fine bulgur for çiğ köfte
  • 2 cups hot water
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste (pepper paste can also be used)
  • 1 medium onion
  • 1 Turkish coffee cup of olive oil
  • 1 Turkish coffee cup of isot (Urfa pepper)
  • 1 Turkish coffee cup of chili flakes (can be reduced)
  • 1 teaspoon of salt (adjust if your paste is unsalted)
  • 2 tablespoons of pomegranate molasses
  • Juice of 1 lemon

Steps

  1. Start by soaking the fine bulgur with hot water.
  2. Let it sit for about 10 minutes to soften and absorb the water.
  3. Meanwhile, prepare the ingredients. Grate the onion using the fine side of a grater.
  4. Place the soaked bulgur in a food processor. If your processor has a plastic blade, use that for better texture (to avoid over-mashing), but a metal blade also works.
  5. Add the grated onion, tomato paste, isot, chili flakes, olive oil, pomegranate molasses, and lemon juice.
  6. Season with salt according to taste.
  7. Close the lid of the processor and blend on the lowest setting.
  8. Let the food processor run for at least 10 minutes. Gradually mix so that the top ingredients are pulled into the blend, mimicking the kneading process.
  9. After 10 minutes, check the consistency — if it looks like a smooth, dough-like paste, it's ready.
  10. While the köfte is processing, you can prepare extras like lettuce, lemon wedges, or ayran for serving.
  11. Transfer the mixture to a bowl, shape by hand into traditional köfte forms, and place on a serving plate.
